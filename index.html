Our Mission

Our mission is to tackle governance as a great challenge through Women Integration as a great opportunity. We strive to build legacies of empowered communities, fostering transparency, accountability, and sustainable development.

Discover Africa

Africa is a continent rich in diversity, culture, and resilience. One fascinating community we highlight is the Hadza people of Tanzania, one of the last hunter-gatherer tribes in the world. Known for their deep connection to the land and traditional way of life, the Hadza offer valuable lessons in sustainability and community harmony. Through our work, we draw inspiration from such indigenous groups to promote inclusive governance and cultural preservation across the continent.

Help Lab

As part of our commitment to community support, we visited Girimpuhwe Clinic, our local community hospital. During this visit, we provided essential day-to-day supplies to help ensure the clinic can continue delivering quality healthcare services to the residents of Gashaki and surrounding areas. This initiative reflects our dedication to addressing immediate needs while working towards long-term governance improvements.

Governance Insights

We had the privilege of meeting with the Director of Good Governance in Kigali, where we gained invaluable insights into the governance sector. The discussion highlighted key aspects of women empowerment and the significant efforts undertaken by the Rwandan government to promote inclusive governance. We learned about innovative strategies for accountability, participatory decision-making, and sustainable development, which have inspired our approach to community building and alignment with Rwanda's Vision 2050.

The Gashaki Community

Our community of choice is the Gashaki sector in Musanze, Rwanda. The sector faces governance challenges which we seek to address through women empowerment—promoting strengthened participation, accountability, and local economic development. By focusing on this vibrant community, we aim to create lasting positive change.

Problem Statement

In Gashaki Sector of Musanze District, weak community participation and limited accountability continue to hinder effective local governance and sustainable development. Although Rwanda has made significant progress through decentralisation, studies (MKU Repository, 2024) reveal that citizens in Gashaki are rarely involved in planning, budgeting, and implementing local projects. As a result, service delivery remains poor, infrastructure underdeveloped, and household incomes low. The problem mainly affects rural residents, youth, and local leaders who feel excluded from important decision-making processes, leading to mistrust and dependency on external aid. It mostly occurs during community project cycles and public consultations, especially between 2022 and 2025, when local planning sessions take place. The main causes include low public awareness, weak coordination, and limited institutional capacity at the local level. Addressing this issue is essential to promote transparency, accountability, and citizen empowerment, aligning Gashaki’s development with Rwanda’s Vision 2050 for inclusive governance.

Our Solution: The Gashaki Governance Hub

The Gashaki Governance Hub will empower women and youth through capacity building, participatory forums, and digital tools that promote accountability and local decision-making. By strengthening governance structures and inclusion, we aim to foster transparency, active citizenship, and sustainable development aligned with Rwanda’s Vision 2050.
